GA4 Ecommerce Analytics — 02

Full-Funnel GA4 Analysis: Diagnosing a 90.3% Cart Abandonment Rate Across Channels & Devices

GA4Looker StudioAttribution AnalysisFunnel Analysis
Business Problem: The Google Merchandise Store was generating $526K in revenue across 319K sessions — but 90.3% of users who added items to cart never completed a purchase. Traffic was healthy. Conversion was broken.
Objective: Identify where the funnel was leaking, which channels were driving buyers vs. browsers, and whether last-click attribution was misrepresenting channel value.
Key Insight: The biggest drop-off was not at checkout — it was at the product page. 75.5% of users who viewed a product never added it to cart, pointing to a product page problem rather than a checkout problem. Desktop converted at 8.11% vs. 2.31% on mobile — a 3.5x gap signaling significant mobile UX issues. Under last-click attribution, Email and Organic Shopping appeared small; data-driven attribution revealed both were significantly undervalued, with Organic Shopping's revenue contribution jumping 17.31%.
$526K revenue · 20.54% conversion rate · 90-day analysis · 3-page Looker Studio dashboard
Live dashboard
Analysis

Conducted a full GA4 audit of the Google Merchandise Store demo account across a 90-day period. Built a funnel exploration from view_item → add_to_cart → begin_checkout → purchase with device-level and channel-level breakdowns. Compared last-click vs. data-driven attribution models to identify misattributed channel credit. Pulled traffic acquisition data across 10 channels analyzing sessions, engagement rate, session key event rate, and revenue side by side.

Key Findings

Direct traffic dominated sessions (65.6%) but had the lowest engagement rate (21.68%) and worst key event rate (11.71%) — high volume, low quality. Organic Shopping had just 0.24% of sessions but a 74.87% key event rate — the highest converting channel by far. Paid Search generated $46K in revenue from only 2.43% of sessions, the highest revenue-per-session of any channel. Data-driven attribution revealed Email (+9.92%) and Cross-network (+10.66%) were systematically undercredited under last-click.

Recommendations

Prioritize mobile UX improvements — specifically product pages and checkout flow on mobile, where a 1% conversion lift would represent significant revenue given mobile's session share. Shift budget reporting to data-driven attribution before making channel investment decisions. Protect Email and Organic Shopping budgets — cutting them based on last-click data would be a costly mistake. Investigate Unassigned traffic (8.92% of sessions, 5.71% engagement) as a data quality gap diluting overall metrics.

Audience Research & Data Analysis

Longitudinal Audience Study: Tracking Behavioral Shifts Across a 4-Year Crisis

PythonSQLSQLitePandasTableauLongitudinal Analysis
Business Problem: 398,884 student records across 4 years — each inconsistently structured, with no unified view of how mental health shifted before, during, and after COVID.
Objective: Identify which student populations were most at risk and whether institutional support resources aligned with actual need.
Key Insight: Mental health symptoms peaked in Early Recovery (2021–22) — not during COVID. Universities scaled back therapy exactly when students needed it most.
Depression hit 73% · Academic impairment stayed above 79% across all 4 years
Live dashboard GitHub →
Analysis

Built a Python + SQL pipeline that loaded 4 raw CSVs into SQLite, standardized inconsistent columns, and unified everything into a 398,884-row master table. Five SQL queries answered core research questions. Results visualized in Tableau.

Recommendation

Tracking therapy access is not the same as tracking academic recovery. Universities need longitudinal KPIs that follow students post-crisis, not just during it.

Impact

Identified highest-risk groups: non-binary students (84% depression, 95% academic impairment) and men least likely to access therapy (30%) despite 51% depression rates. Surfaced a resource allocation gap with direct policy implications.
