NovaStyle AI Marketing Intelligence System

PythonScikit-learnOpenAI APIStreamlitPandasMatplotlib
Business Problem

NovaStyle was spending across five channels with no reliable view of which were driving conversions — and which were draining budget.

Objective

Predict which customers would respond to campaigns, identify underperforming channels, and generate strategic recommendations automatically.

Analysis

Built a Python + Scikit-learn classification model on customer behavior, RFM data, and campaign history. Layered OpenAI API to generate plain-language recommendations from model outputs.

Key Insights
  • Email had the highest campaign acceptance rate across all 5 channels
  • Instagram significantly underperformed — flagged as primary budget drain
  • Customers 60+ are the highest-value segment by total spend
  • Top response predictors: Total Spend, Recency, Income — 86% model accuracy
Recommendation
Shift Instagram budget toward Email and TikTok. Target campaigns by predicted response likelihood — not broad demographic buckets.
Impact
Predictive model enables targeting by response score instead of guesswork. AI-generated recommendations give non-technical stakeholders clear direction — no analyst required to interpret the output.

Retention & Growth Analysis — 02

Retention Audit: Diagnosing Drop-Off Behind Strong Revenue (Project)

GA4ExcelRetention AnalysisCase Study
Business Problem: Chewy's revenue was up 80% YoY — but Android traffic was down 31% in the same period. One number was doing all the talking.
Objective: Determine whether mobile drop-off was a device-specific UX issue or a broader retention problem.
Key Insight: Android engagement time was declining alongside traffic — a mobile experience problem, not a seasonal dip. Revenue growth was masking a retention floor that would compound as acquisition scaled.
Recommendation → Fix mobile retention before scaling acquisition spend.
Analysis

Analyzed acquisition, engagement, and device-level performance data in GA4 and Excel. Compared returning-user behavior across platforms and channels to identify where drop-off was concentrated.

Key Finding

Android traffic declined ~31% YoY alongside lower engagement time on mobile. Revenue growth was masking a retention problem that would compound as acquisition scaled — a leaky floor, not a foundation.

Impact

Surfaced a retention risk buried under top-line growth. Recommendation: prioritize mobile UX improvements before pushing acquisition further, or risk scaling into a problem that erodes unit economics.

Audience Research & Data Analysis — 03

Longitudinal Audience Study: Tracking Behavioral Shifts Across a 4-Year Crisis

PythonSQLSQLitePandasTableauLongitudinal Analysis
Business Problem: 398,884 student records across 4 years — each inconsistently structured, with no unified view of how mental health shifted before, during, and after COVID.
Objective: Identify which student populations were most at risk and whether institutional support resources aligned with actual need.
Key Insight: Mental health symptoms peaked in Early Recovery (2021–22) — not during COVID. Universities scaled back therapy exactly when students needed it most.
Depression hit 73% · Academic impairment stayed above 79% across all 4 years
Analysis

Built a Python + SQL pipeline that loaded 4 raw CSVs into SQLite, standardized inconsistent columns, and unified everything into a 398,884-row master table. Five SQL queries answered core research questions. Results visualized in Tableau.

Recommendation

Tracking therapy access is not the same as tracking academic recovery. Universities need longitudinal KPIs that follow students post-crisis, not just during it.

Impact

Identified highest-risk groups: non-binary students (84% depression, 95% academic impairment) and men least likely to access therapy (30%) despite 51% depression rates. Surfaced a resource allocation gap with direct policy implications.

Lifecycle & Engagement Campaign — 05

High-Touch Retention Campaign: Driving Emotional Engagement at Scale

Experience DesignProject ManagementAdobe Suite
Business Problem: 800+ students lost their in-person graduation to COVID-19 — after already losing a year of campus life. A Zoom link wasn't a meaningful substitute.
Objective: Create a physically tangible graduation experience that made students feel recognized — without an in-person event.
Key Insight: Physical keepsakes + a structured virtual experience with department breakout rooms addressed the emotional gap a livestream link alone couldn't fill.
250+ organic shares · 50+ direct emails from students and families
Analysis

Mapped what students typically experience around graduation — farewell events, department celebrations, time with faculty. Identified which moments had the highest emotional weight and could be recreated physically or virtually.

Recommendation & Action

Coordinated personalized graduation boxes delivered to 800+ graduates — containing a "Pat on the Back" keepsake booklet, customized cookies, graduation props, a cap, and a virtual batch meetup with department breakout rooms.

Impact

250+ organic shares and 50+ direct positive emails from students and families — that volume of spontaneous response signals genuine emotional resonance, not just satisfaction with logistics.
